Home >> 10 Excellent JavaScript YouTube Channels

I'm a big fan of free Web Development learning resources, including the countless hours of videos that can be found on the YouTube platform.

In no particular order, here are ten excellent YouTube channels that focus on using JavaScript for Web Development.

Caleb Curry

Lots of media on Web Development centered topics, including Machine Learning; as well as how they all integrate as study materials.

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Chau Codes

Relatable journey of a self-taught Web Developer, something that is valuable for newbies as well as those who will have jobs soon

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

CodingPhase

A video journal styled YouTube channel from someone who made his own money with Web Development, as well as many other skills; a self-made man who seems to love technology and programming, relatable content

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Dorian Develops

A quickly growing YouTube channel run by a Web Developer who is breaking new ground in terms of value and content; I enjoy his videos and his relatively sarcastic presentation style

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Fredrik Christenson

Personal responses to viewer questions, from the perspective of an experienced programmer who loves what he does; his videos are often combinations of video from his walks and audio containing his advice, interesting format

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Free Code Camp

Probably the biggest Web Development YouTube channel shared in this video, has information about an ever increasing number of Computer Science topics; IMO, this channel should be taken seriously for anyone looking to supplement any other kind of learning about code

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Low Level JavaScript

Uniquely detailed information about what’s possible with JavaScript, casually creating software that goes beyond what I ever thought possible with the JavaScript language

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

RealToughCandy

Sarcastic and spirited advice for programmers, from a corporate programmer; this is someone who likes having a job and wants to help other programmers get jobs too

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Traversy Media

Comprehensive and in-depth videos about Web Development from a team of people, one of whom I’ve met, and their approach certainly is a community-driven philosophy; great stuff here

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

Tyler Potts

Frequent livestreams, enables some great feedback from him and his audience on Web Development projects; will review your portfolio live on his program for others to see and to receive his commentary

Here is a link to their YouTube channel.

In Conclusion

What makes these YouTube channels unique is their commitment to providing high quality content for their audiences.
